Wait… money can grow?!

Yeah. I want to show you how.

What's a stock?

When a company wants to grow, they sell tiny slices of themselves called stocks.

If you own one, you own a little sliver of the company. If the company gets more valuable, your slice does too.

One stock = one slice.

What if you'd bought Netflix in 2010?

$100 in 2010 → $10,429 today

…or Nvidia?

$100 in 2010 → $57,040 today

That's the same company, just worth way more because lots of people now want a slice.

But… most stocks don't do that.

In 2010, you could just as easily have picked Blockbuster — and lost almost everything.

🥚 🧺 ❌

Don't put all your eggs in one basket.

Here's a safer way: buy a slice of everything.

$100 in 1990 → $2,160 today

The S&P 500 is a bundle of the 500 biggest companies. If one company fails, the others pick up the slack.

The secret? Time.

Money grows when you give it time.
